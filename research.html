Research & Technical Interests

  • Radiation detection and signal processing for rare-event searches
  • Detector instrumentation, noise characterization and sensor design
  • Charge transport simulation and waveform analysis (Garfield++, Magboltz, ROOT)
  • Development of proportional gas detectors and liquid noble TPCs
  • Neutrino and dark-matter physics instrumentation

Selected Projects & Experience

  • Graduate Research Assistant, Case Western Reserve University (2020–2025)
    Designed and built gas-based proportional counters with analog and digital readout chains; performed signal processing and noise characterization; simulated charge transport to optimize electric fields; and led detector calibration campaigns for high-resolution measurements.
  • Summer Intern, Update International (2024)
    Conducted comparative evaluations of laser–photodiode systems for biomedical imaging prototypes and collaborated with engineering teams to validate precision sensing components.
  • Teaching Assistant, Case Western Reserve University (2019–2021)
    Designed laboratory experiments, graded assignments and mentored more than 200 undergraduate students; developed hybrid and virtual lab formats during pandemic disruptions.
  • Sub-wavelength light manipulation
    Investigated lens-focused phase extraction techniques to control light intensity distributions beyond the diffraction limit as part of my master’s thesis at IIT Guwahati.
  • Analog signal generator
    Designed a triangle-wave oscillator using a 741 op-amp to explore signal generation and analog electronics principles (IIT Guwahati).
