Hi, there!

I am a PhD candidate in Physics at Case Western Reserve University with more than five years of experience in radiation detection, signal processing and detector instrumentation. I am passionate about developing precision measurement systems for rare-event searches and diagnostic applications. My work spans waveform analysis, charge transport simulation and data-driven calibration using C++, Python and MATLAB. I enjoy collaborating across disciplines, documenting results and mentoring students. I aim to apply and broaden these skills to solve real-world detector and instrumentation challenges.

Education

  • PhD in Physics, Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland USA (2020–present)
    JN Tata Scholar – thesis research on proportional gas detectors for rare-event searches.
  • MSc in Physics, Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India (2017–2019)
    INSPIRE Scholar – master’s thesis on sub-wavelength light manipulation via lens-focused phase extraction.
  • BSc in Physics (Honours), SGTB Khalsa College, University of Delhi, India (2014–2017)
    INSPIRE Scholar – awarded Best Female Student in Physics.
